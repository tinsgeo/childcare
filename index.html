Taking a Break, Restoring Balance

Every Parent Deserves Time To Recharge

Caring for a child with special needs or a disability often means you are on the clock 24/7. As parents, the opportunity to have a night out or just spend a few hours alone is usually few and far between or non-existent. On top of the usual obstacles of reliable or affordable childcare, you probably also find yourself asking.

  • Is anyone available and willing to take on the additional mental and physical load that comes with caring for my child?
  • Can someone genuinely be trusted with the complexities of my child’s care?
  • Will my child be able to communicate their needs to a sitter?

Respite Care Designed With Your Child in Mind

No matter your child’s diagnosis or story, our Respite Care services are designed to accommodate their needs for up to several hours.

  • Every child is assigned a screened and trained volunteer. The volunteer spends the entire evening playing one-on-one with the child and is directed never to leave their side.
  • Children can access safe and developmentally appropriate crafts, toys, and outdoor play spaces when the weather is nice, and they can spend their time how they choose!
  • A KOTM staff member and a volunteer supervisor are on-site at all times. Children who require medication or nursing care are assigned to a night with a nurse. (Six out of seven of our current respite staff are nurses)
  • We know separation can often be difficult for children, but we have you covered there, too! While the first visit may be emotional, do not worry; we will find something your little one loves and get them excited about coming to Respite Care.

Join Our Respite Care Program

It would be our honor to serve your family with Respite Care services at KOTM. Here are the next steps to joining the program.

First, Let’s Make Sure We’re a Fit

To serve as many families as possible to the best of our ability in a safe environment, children who are eligible for Respite Care are…

  • Between 0-12 years old.
  • Diagnosed with a special need that is an intellectual, behavioral, or physical disability.
  • Able to be at respite until 9:00 p.m. without it disturbing their bedtime routine.
  • Not on 100% oxygen or excessively medically fragile.
  • Free of a history of sexually inappropriate or aggressive behavior.
  • In a family that does not already have resources that provide them with a break from caregiving.

Next, Fill Out an Application

Please fill out one application per family; you can add multiple children if you have several with special needs. Due to the high volume of families needing respite services, there is often a waitlist. We will let you know that we have received your application.

Apply for Respite Care

Finally, Get Ready To Party!

Once a spot opens up, we will schedule an intake interview. We will review any behavioral and medical needs regarding your child. Either the nurse or our behavioral center manager will develop a plan to handle your child’s needs in a positive, loving way. After a few visits, many of our Respite kids tell their parents that they wait all month for Respite because they have so much fun!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Respite Care?

Respite Care offers temporary relief to primary caregivers, providing short-term, professional care for children with special needs.

Who are the caregivers?

Our caregivers are trained and screened volunteers overseen by both a nurse and a KOTM staff member experienced in handling the specific needs of children with disabilities and delays.

What does Respite Care cost?

Our evening program is free to families and funded through generous donations.

How often can my child attend Respite Care?

Because we have a waiting list, we currently offer one night per month to each family. There is an on-call list for families waiting for services.

How are my child’s toileting needs met at Respite Care?

For toileting needs, we have children of all ages who are completely dependent and need diaper changes. We also have children who are completely independent. When a child requires a diaper change or needs to use the restroom independently, two people are always present—a member of our staff and a volunteer. Children are allowed their privacy based on their ability to use the restroom.

Are typical siblings eligible to attend Respite Care nights?

Our program is limited to children with behavioral or physical disabilities.
